Risotto alla Milanese

Ingredients:

  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 1/2 cups Arborio rice
  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 1/2 tsp saffron threads, soaked in 2 tbsp warm water
  • 1/2 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook until softened and translucent, about 4-5 minutes.
  2. Add the Arborio rice to the pot and stir to coat the grains with butter. Cook for about 2 minutes until the rice is lightly toasted.
  3. Pour in the white wine and cook until the wine is mostly absorbed by the rice.
  4. Meanwhile, in a separate pot, bring the broth to a simmer over low heat. Keep the broth warm while making the risotto.
  5. Begin adding the warm broth to the rice, one ladleful at a time, stirring frequently. Wait until the liquid is mostly absorbed before adding more broth. Continue this process until the rice is creamy and cooked through, about 18-20 minutes.
  6. Once the rice is tender and creamy, stir in the saffron with its soaking water, Parmesan cheese, and season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Remove the risotto from heat and let it rest for a couple of minutes before serving. Serve hot, garnished with additional Parmesan if desired.
